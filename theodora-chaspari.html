Theodora Chaspari's CV

chaspari@usc.edu




Theodora's research focuses on developing methods for representing psychophysiological signals, investigating their co-evolution between individuals across time and analyzing their association with observed behavioral aspects of interaction. She works on methods to reliably remove noise artifacts and measure signal characteristics. Her interests also involve ways to quantify the coordination patterns of bio-signals between people. She is currently a PhD student in Signal Analysis and Interpretation Laboratory (SAIL) at USC.
